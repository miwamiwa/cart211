Mind vs Machine


Mind vs Machine

Brian Christian

Brian Christian writes a compelling narrative about his experience at the Loebner Prize competition and his anticipation regarding the event. I found it interesting to find out about the event, and to read about his thoughts and research on artificial intelligence. Moreso than the narrative itself I must say.

He talks about what makes humans unique and goes on with some marxiam stuff about selfhood, and how computers are able to do everything that a human can do and more.
He seems to reference Stallman when he asks if the computer free us "from having to do a nonhuman activity, liberating us into a more human life" which I thought was interesting. Often the talk returns to questions about empathy, I suppose the theme of dehumanisation is important to Christian.

I thought it was also interesting to read about the early (1975) Eliza project and the reactions of the people of the time, who are amazed by the quality of conversation they are getting from a rudimentary artificial intelligence. I feel like I'm used to reading automated messages on social media, and public devices around the city all the time, and I think I've developed a certain awareness of some generic automated messages..
It does make you wonder how long until I get fooled consistantly into thinking an AI is actually a human being. I suppose they could be conviently dumbed down??

I thought the story about the swearing AI was pretty amazing. Also inspiring. There's an easy way to make a conversation with a àcomputer more lively right there (the applications of which are endless).

Loved the mention of cleverbot, which I remember messing around with in highschool. It sort of put Christian's end-of-the-world tone into perspective for me, since as I recall Cleverbot can't follow a conversation at all, only answer one-liners.
